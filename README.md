# Personal-Finance-Analysis
Analyzed 12-month personal spend across Bills, Food, Living &amp; Travel using Power BI. Tracked ₹280K income vs ₹210K expenses, uncovering seasonal cash flow gaps, a 25% savings rate, and ₹33K in annual coffee spend. Includes daily transaction trends, sub-category breakdown &amp; account-level analysis.

## Key Findings
- Savings Rate — Annual income of $0.28M vs expenses of $0.21M yields a 25% savings rate, with net positive of ~$70K — healthy but constrained by income volatility across months
- Category Spend Parity — All four categories (Bills $37K, Living $36K, Food $34K, Travel $33K) fall within a $4K band, indicating no single runaway category but a uniform overspending pattern across the board
- First-Year Cash Flow Red Months — January (-$5K) and November (-$1K) recorded negative net positions, driven by stable expenses against dipping income — revealing absence of a cash buffer strategy
- Peak vs Trough Income Gap — May recorded highest income at $38K vs November low of $12K, a $26K swing in a single year indicating high income volatility with no corresponding expense adjustment
- Daily Transaction Volatility — Intra-month cash flow swings of nearly $27K (peak +$14K to trough -$13K) within a single month signal critical need for a liquidity float
- Coffee Spend Revelation — $33K annual spend on coffee nearly matches Travel ($33K) and is only $4K below the highest category (Bills $37K) — the single most surprising sub-category finding
- Credit Card Dominance — 36% of total debit flows through credit card vs 33% checking and 31% savings, indicating default reliance on credit which carries compounding risk if not cleared monthly
- Electronic Check Behaviour — Month-to-month manual payment habits mirror high-friction billing patterns, suggesting absence of automated financial discipline in payment management
- Entertainment vs Utilities Parity — Entertainment spend equals Utilities at $37K, raising a strategic question about lifestyle vs necessity spend allocation
- Sub-Category Compression — All six sub-categories (Utilities, Entertainment, Transport, Rent, Groceries, Coffee) fall within a $4K range ($33K–$37K), confirming spend is distributed habitually rather than intentionally
